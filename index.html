2. Class Summary

Shadow is a mobile close-quarters bunny with a potent versatility; she excels when playing flexibly, due to Shackles. Many of her effects are unique to her own or inspirations from other classes and effects.

Shadow is best characterized by having:

  • Decent-damage and short GCD (1.0s) abilities
    • Close-Range multi-hit Primary and Secondary abilities
    • Secondary having a leap, similar to Heavyblade’s (HB) Special
    • Special that hits all enemies on short CD
    • Defensives with damage and GCD
  • Shares the fastest base movement speed (m/s) with Assassin (Sin)
  • Shackles status effect

2.2. Shackles

Shadow’s Shackles locks up one of her attacks1 from use on any ability usage. This is independent of any Magicklock trinket effects, cooldowns or item effects (e.g. Lefthand Cast.)

[Author] As well, this can freeze up “input queues” where otherwise, on other characters, you could be able to hold multiple attacks down.

2.3. Inspirations from other Classes and Loot

Shadow gains some distinct effects that appear in other classes, namely:

  • S2’s range and charge effect like Dancer’s Secondary
  • G3 granting the Shadowcry buff, similar to Dancer’s def
  • O2 granting Shadowcurse debuff, similar to Spellsword’s (SS) def
  • G1 granting 100 damage 25% of the time to all enemies, is practically a Brightstorm Spear and Darkstorm Knife

2.4. Gacha ability

E3 grants Shadow common access to, often loot-only or class-exclusive, effects like Berserk, Sap and Blackstrike. This distinctly allows Shadow to build into debuffs, multi-hit (on Special); or support most Shadow builds.

  • See Shadow (rns.miraheze.org) for exhaustive list of effects.

3. Strategies

3.1. Damage Rotations

3.1.1. Base (Outskirts/Geode)

On base kit, in order of availibility:

  • If at ~6s/21s, N4
  • N3
  • N1
  • N4
  • N2

For regular encounters, defeating them by 22.5s will score 9 gold, for up to 47 gold on Random runs. This may allow you to buy two gems immediately or an extra pot.

The hitbox covered by N1 and N4 are equal. N2 doesn’t hit as tall as either, but hits almost as wide. 2

3.1.2. Ruby Leap Archetypes - R2/R4

The rotation typically goes:

  • R2 / Leap Secondary repeatedly

Stable on low-roll lines, archetype is nigh consistent for single-target DPS early and late.

For normal-sized enemies, play near the distance leap can be enabled. With and/or , while returning towards this leap border—:

  1. If Secondary is available, I move just outside to enable leap and use it.
  2. If Secondary gets Shackles, I may move inside to Primary/Defensive (to hit Stella’s hitbox below); or just Special.

Figure 1: Distances in Rabbitleaps (lp) with Stella

3.1.3. Auto-proc + oGCD’s (S1/G1/R4 + S3/S4)

Shadow, at base kit, has very symmetrical damage across her abilities, at expense of being GCD-limited. Any ability that gets auto-activates or avoids contesting GCD is valued highly

  • S1 and G1 allows Shadow to avoid clicking Primary in previous rotations, while supplementing extra damage
    • R4 dealing on Secondary focused-builds
  • S3 and S4, as oGCD’s, while listed equal damage to base, can be thought of adding +200 damage ( N1) every click.

In contrast, Druid is much more asymmetrical: her abilities are duration/CD-based; with one Secondary, she deals 720 total damage unlike Shadow’s 180.

While Druid may spend the most time clicking Primary’s, often it is not her main damage source.

4. Multiplayer Notes

4.1. Avoiding leap PVP

For damage/consistency, see Ruby Leap Archetypes - R2/R4.

4.2. Practices on Targetting

Here are some good suggestions on using the targetting controls Nearest Target and Change Target.

If you’re in a fight with multiple enemies (Sanctum/Churchmouse), and you do not want to leap:

  • identify your current target (and if they’re low health)
  • pressing Nearest Target prior to Secondary
  • pressing Nearest Target after pressing Change Target

Change Target, while self-described, also allows you to slow your character or attack the “centre” (in-between) of targets:

  • alternatively, “tapping” movement keys for micromovements
  • slowing down your rotation
  • not holding attack buttons for prolonged periods

4.3. Leap Notification

In Options>More Options, you can toggle and customize Mini Hotbar.

An accent colour will border the Secondary if leap is enabled, dependant on Shadow palette.

6. Gems Tier List

Tier placing prioritizes “lines” and WR consistency. Shadow, by gem design, should buy a gem first gem first shop, most are damage positive.

  • If avoiding leap, one can consider buying S2 and G2; avoid O4.
  • Generally, Special damage gems, besides S3, are not a prio pick due to their GCD, CD and low base damage.
  • If on Subterra Sanctum or Churchmouse Streets first stage, consider G1, G4 or special gem.
  • O2, G3 is great for MP, they’re practically SS’s Superhex and Dancer’s Warcry respectively.

6.1. S: Mainstays

R4, R2 and R1, each alone, can do +30-100% DPS without external support early, though more difficult to work with due to iframe req.

You’re looking for Wavestrike, Flash-, Flow- (only R1 and R2); Blackstrike, Haste buffs, and applicable ability damage bonuses.

6.2. A: Good

6.3. B: Alternative

G2 is a risky early buy without S3 and S4 / G4; or, deliberate, builds where you focus Special or %’s.

6.4. C: Viable

[Author] Although E1 should be fine, in most games, I skip it. The long m/s slow and uncheatble 2s GCD feels too demanding (excluding lining up Flash- or Blackstrike buff.)

R3 is likely the best gem for spamming Special damage out or proccing effects like Book of Cheats or Saltwater Staff.

6.5. D: Ultra-niche

O3 and E4 close too many future lines for its damage at base kit.
